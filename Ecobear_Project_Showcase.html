Context

Ecobear was my group's submission to the IDEO x Invention Corps Spring 2024 Designathon held on the University of California, Berkeley campus. We won 2nd place from a total of 20 teams.

Our prompt for the challenge was:

“How might we bring the little joys of gentle and emerging tech to empower communities with sustainable living?”

Preliminary Research & Conceptualization - Defining Ecobear

Identifying What Aspect of Sustainable Living to Tackle

My group and I took the first hour and half to research and started to jot down ideas using Fig-Jam.

We first wanted to identify avenues where sustainable living is applicable. and landed on groccery shopping based on shared interests and potential for impact via research we conducted.

From our research, we found that the average U.S. household emits 8 metric tons of CO2 per year, which is significantly above the sustainable target of 2 tons of CO2 per year set by the Paris Climate Agreement. As a result we wanted our app to empower users to make sustainable choices while gorccery shopping by selecting organic items, items with recyclable packaging, and items with lower associated carbon production to help reduce individual carbon footprint.

Picking An Emerging Technology

We decided to utilize AI for the emerging technology portion, as our team is most familiar with it and can reasonably present the functionality in Figma.

Through discussions, we expanded on the idea to include a live mascot that provides affirmations for users' sustainable choices and acts as a resource for users to ask questions while shopping. Additionally, it would calculate the total impact of their choices, allowing users to see and understand upfront how their actions affect the environment.

This concept is supported by psychology research we conducted, which shows that:

  • Positive feedback enhances positive emotional responses and releases dopamine, making individuals feel good about their actions.
  • Consistent exposure to cues such as prompts and affirmations our AI mascot would provide, help reinforce habits.

Mission Statement

With these foundations, we landed on EcoBear’s mission:

An online grocery platform that promotes sustainability and enables consumers to make eco-friendly choices effortlessly, transforming everyday shopping into a greener future.

Key Functionality

With our mission statement defined, we identified key functionalities for the prototype. This ensured our features addressed the entire prompt and helped us focus given our limited time.

  • AI Chatbox: An interactive mascot, specialized to answer questions on sustainable grocery shopping (similar to ChatGPT).
  • Sustainability Filtering: Allows users to find and select items that meet their needs, such as organic and plastic-free products.
  • Impact Summarization Provides users with a summary of their total sustainable impact from making particular grocery choices.
  • Affirmations: Offers positive feedback when users take key actions, such as adding their first organic item to their cart.

Mascot Design & Logo

With a general idea of how our app would look like, some of my team members went on to design our mascot: BareBear! We wanted a cute, inviting mascot to emulate the “cheerleader” aspect we were going for that the bear would provide with its affirmations. Full-credit to my amazing teammates for designing the logo and mascot!

High-Fi-Prototypes

While some members worked on the Mascot Design, I led the design for the high-fidelity screens and linking the flows. With only two hours remaining, designing an organized and polished (to the extent possible) was a challenge, but I was super happy with how the app turned out given the constraint.

Home Page Screen

The home page immediately offers choices for users to kickstart their sustainable shopping. Users can also use the search bar from here to manually find items.

Shopping Screen

Users can filter items out based on sustainable categories and according to their needs.

First Sustainable Choice Affirmation Pop-Up

When the user makes their first sustainable choice, BareBear will pop up and provide the user an affirmation! User is free to exit out after.

Sustainable Impact Screen

Along with seeing the items added and total price for checkout, users will receive an impact summary from Bare Bear, showcasing how purchasing specific items improved the environment for the better!

AI Mascot Chatbox Screen

User can asks any question to the Mascot AI to aid their shopping experience. The AI would be trained specifically on sustainable groccery shopping, food-waste, food production, and related topics to ensure accurate information.

Final Results

Following our presentation, we were ranked 2nd place out of 20 teams! We also got some free swag and merch as additional rewards :)

Reflection

This was my first time designing under time pressure. It was an extremely rewarding experience, and I enjoyed collaborating with my team as we shared our design perspectives to create a finished final product.

From this expeirence, I had two main take aways:

  • Being concise is critical: With only 2 minutes to present, it was important for us to cover the main point: the app. Our idea was simple but had unique functionality. We found that other teams spent too much time on background and research, leaving them insufficient time to fully present their deliverables.
  • The Importance of taking time to ideate: A key reason for our group's success was taking the time to finalize our idea rather than rushing into building the app immediately. This thoughtful approach allowed us to create a more cohesive and well-thought-out product.

Overall this designathon, was an experience that showcased what it is like to work collaboratively on a design process. It provided me with valuable learnings on how to successfully create designs in the future as well.
