SNACKPASS

A personal project focused on improving user satisfaction with Snackpass's reward system, while introducing and refining gamification features to amplify its value proposition!

Quick Summary

Problem:

Snackpass's current reward system and gamifcation features are not incentivizing enough to keep users engaged for long periods.

Solution:

Designed "Favorite Store Feature" to increase accessibility of reward system by promoting efficient point gifting, and link gamifcation features to to primary functions of the app: food ordering and pickup.

Outcome:

Successfully had solutions reviewed by Co-Founder of Snackpass for future consideration. Check out the full deck I shared: Snackpass Deck.

Context

Snackpass is a social food-pick-up app aimed at university students.

Users order ahead for pickup, earn loyalty points for discounts, and can gift friends. The app includes group-ordering discounts, in-app discounts, and a Venmo-like feed. Its gamification feature, "Chicken Coop" rewards users with coins for gifting, which can be exchanged for accessories to dress up virtual pet chickens.

Problem Statement

"Too many orders required to receive free rewards to incentivize me to take advantage of it.‚Äù - Surveyed Snackpass User.

After three years of using Snackpass, I've only managed to claim free rewards once due to the unattainable and slow nature of the current reward system. Consequently, I've found myself using Snackpass less frequently since there's little motivation unless it's purely convenient. Additionally, the "Chicken Coop" gamification feature feels disconnected and lacks appeal for users, like myself, that are uninterested in dressing up virtual chickens.

Thus, I asked:

How would I increase accessibility and statisfaction of the rewards system and modify and implement gamification elements to help Snackpass continue to set itself apart?

Validation

Before developing any higher-fidelity prototypes, I surveyed 50+ Snackpass users to validate pain-points and solutions I had in mind. The survey was primarily aimed at understanding user gifting behavior, reward system attainability and opinions on the chicken coop feature.

Below are some main statistics and comments regarding user gifting behavior and the reward system:

  • 58.1% of users said they would want points from friends for a few specific stores they frequent.
  • 66.7% of users said Snackpass only feels slightly social.
  • "Too many orders required to recieve free rewards to incetivize me to take advantage of it."
  • 73.8% of users said they rarely use the Chicken Coop feature and see no benefit to it.
  • "The Chicken Coop feature feels out of place and there is nothing I care about to use the coins for."

From these statistics I was able to get the following takeaways:

  • Users prefer earning points from specific stores and like making progress towards them.
  • They want a more attainable rewards system.
  • Opportunities exist to use gamification features to improve engagement.
  • Update the Chicken Coop feature to be relevant to core functionality of the app: food-delivery and rewards.

Solutions Process: Wireframes

With the takeaways in mind I went on to wire-frame solutions that aimed to solve the pain-points I discovered. This lead me to three components of the project: Favorite Store, Leaderboards, Redesigning Chicken Coop.

Favorite Store Wireframes

Favorite Store Wireframe #1

Favorite Store Wireframe #2

STRATEGIC REASONING:

This feature allow users to set up a "Favorite Store" from their profile settings that they would like to get points from (Wireframe #1). This would then be communicated to all the user's friends during their chance to send a gift (Wireframe #2).

The goal is to generate more efficient point gifting and allow users to get points for stores they care about. Since users indicate they want points from a particular set of stories, this feature would help them progress within the rewards program more easily. Users would be prompted to send points to their friends who specifically request it, allowing them to get free rewards more often.

DESIGN CHOICES (Set-up Flow):

Intuitive Placement: The set-up was intentionally placed in a user's profile settings, since this would feel the most intuitive for any new users wanting to adjust anything that uniquely pertains to them.

Confirmation Messaging: Reduce uncertainty about action user is about to take; prevents them from making mistakes and having to go through process again.

Consistency: I chose to use the Snackpass brand colors as part of the messaging and utilizing its existing "Search" page for restaurants to remain consistent with other pages within the app and to make it easier to implement.

User Flow for Favorite Store Set-Up

DESIGN CHOICES (Gifting Flow):

Indicator: Friends that are requesting points from the store a user is about to gift, is showcased at the very top with a bright brand-consitent indicator, ensuring users see the opportunity to make a decision that will be more impactful.

Highlight Selection: Highlighting the whole section clearly communicates to the user which friend they have selected to make sure they are sending their gift to the right person.

Leaderboard Wireframes

Leaderboard Wireframe #1

Leaderboard Wireframe #2

STRATEGIC REASONING:

Leaderboards traditionally is an effective gamification feature that makes users competiive and compare themselevs with others. Users at top rankings feel a sense of accomplishment and recognition and are motivated to keep their ranks. In addition, non-compeittive users enjoy looking at leaderboards out of curioisity, and consume the information presented by them.

I saw an avenue to implement this in the context of a food-delivery app. Imagine seeing your friends is on the top leaderboard for ordering 50 bobas within the span of 2 months! Likewise, seeing oneself on leaderboard provides motivation to maintain the position.

Thus, the feature's goal is to increase user engagement and social aspect of Snackpass by creating conversations between users and providing them more incentives to engage with the app.

Leaderboard's will achieve this by showcasing rankings of users under specific categories and by store. Users will also have the option to see how they rank around their friends and also all other users within the area.

DESIGN CHOICES:

Faimiliarity: I researched how leaderboards were structured in mobile game apps that commonly rank player performance amongst sets of metrics. Through this, I adjusted the design (Wireframe #1 adjusted to Wireframe #2) for Snackpass's content but the manner in which information is presented is similar to what you would find in gaming/sport mediums, making it concise and familiar.

Highlighting Top Players: I wanted top users to be clearly distinct and recognized, and thus applied a different color gradient from other users at lower ranks.

Intuitive Placement: I put "Leaderboards" within the same area as "Trending", a page on Snackpass where users can see what people are ordering from restaurants currently. "Trending" is currently the only page under the lightning icon. Thus, placement of the Leaderboards under this icon is intuitive since both are data-oriented and informational pages.

Chicken Coop Readjustment

After conducting the user surveys, I decided to make a mind-map to highlight key pain-points of the Chicken Coop Feature and ideate solutions. Primary pain-points from user surveys was the feature lacked relevancy for the rest of the app and there is little incetive for users to interact with the feature

Thus, the following solutions were ideated:

1. Link the Favorite Store feature to Chicken Coop by providing extra progress to hatching chicks if a user gives the points to their friends favorite store.

  • WHY: The feature would be conntected to a core function of the app: ordering and delivering.

2. A new gift-card system called SnackCards that allows users to convert coins collected from chickens into free rewards and discounts.

  • WHY: As the currency can only be gained by interacting with the feature, users are incetivized to use it if they want another method to obtain rewards

3. Leaderboards will have a category for the chicken coop feature such as most chickens hatched.

  • WHY: To allow users to be recognized for interacting with the feature alot and also act as incetives for users that are more interested in competition.

DESIGN CHOICES:

SncakCards: When designing the SnackCards, I used bright-colors and gradients to maintain the "fun" and "social" brand image that Snackpass has established itself with.

Centralizing Features: In adjusting the Chicken Coop feature, I redesigned the page to have all related functions for the chickens‚Äîfrom collecting coins to shopping for new clothes‚Äîall on one page. Users now have access to everything they need front and center rather than navigating through different pages.

Reflection

This was my first experience in making a personal project and taking control of understanding and designing a product to imrpove it! I had a great learning experience in teaching myself Figma and understanding all the nunances behind the software. I also reseach mobile app design techniques and accesibility concepts to make sure that the designs I were making were as feasible as they could be.

An aspect of the project I would have done differently if I had time, is interview more business owners! Users like me are one-side of the coin, so I would want to survey restaurant partners with Snackpass how the rewards system affects their business to see if an more effective middle-ground can be achieved.

Regardless, I am super proud of what I was able to achieve and my ability to learn new techniques and push myself out of my comfort zone. Since this was a personal project, I designed all assets and aspects of the project on my own alongst managing my coursework. However, I kept pushing since I know the end-result of redesigning and thinking strategically on how to improve a product I use often is something I can be proud of!
