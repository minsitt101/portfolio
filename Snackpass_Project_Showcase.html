Context

Snackpass is a social food-pick-up app aimed at university students. Users order ahead for pickup, earn loyalty points for discounts, and can gift friends. The app includes group-ordering discounts, in-app discounts, and a Venmo-like feed. Its gamification feature, "Chicken Coop," rewards users with coins for gifting, which can be exchanged for accessories to dress up virtual pet chickens.

Problem Statement & Goal

"Too many orders required to receive free rewards to incentivize me to take advantage of it.” - Surveyed Snackpass user

After three years of using Snackpass, I've only managed to claim free rewards once due to the unattainable and slow nature of the current reward system. Consequently, I've found myself using Snackpass less frequently since there's little motivation unless it's purely convenient. Additionally, the "Chicken Coop" gamification feature feels disconnected and lacks appeal for users, like myself, that are uninterested in dressing up virtual chickens. To address these issues, I aimed to enhance satisfaction and accessibility by proposing features that help users obtain rewards faster and aimed to adjust current and add new gamification elements to increase user engagement overall to set Snackpass apart from other food pick-up/delivery apps.

Validation

Before developing any higher-fidelity prototypes, I surveyed 50+ Snackpass users to validate pain-points and solutions I had in mind. The survey was primarily aimed at understanding user gifting behavior, reward system attainability and opinions on the chicken coop feature.

Below are some main statistics and comments regarding user gifting behavior and the reward system:

58.1% of users said they would want points from friends for a few specific stores they frequent.

66.7% of users said Snackpass only feels slightly social.

"Too many orders required to recieve free rewards to incetivize me to take advantage of it."

73.8% of users said they rarely use the Chicken Coop feature and see no benefit to it.

"The Chicken Coop feature feels out of place and there is nothing I care about to use the coins for."

From these statistics I was able to get the following takeaways:

  • Users prefer earning points from specific stores and like making progress towards them.
  • They want a more attainable rewards system.
  • Opportunities exist to use gamification features to improve engagement.
  • Update the Chicken Coop feature to be relevant to core functionality of the app: food-delivery and rewards.

Solutions Process: Wireframes

With the takeaways in mind I went on to wire-frame solutions that aimed to solve the pain-points I discovered. This lead me to three components of the project: Favorite Store Feature, Leaderboards, Redesgining Chicken Coop.

Favorite Store Wireframes

Favorite Store Wireframe #1

Favorite Store Wireframe #2

The idea behind this feature is to allow users to set-up in their profile a "Favorite Store" they would like to get points from (Wireframe #2). This would then be communicated to all the user's friends during their chance to send a gift (Wireframe #3). The hope is to generate more efficient point gifting and allow users to get points for stores they care about. This would help make the rewards program more attainable and satisfying as users would be getting rewards for stores they enjoy!

The set-up was intnetionally placed in a user's profile settings, since this would feel the most intuitive for any new users wanting to adjust anything that uniquely pertains to them.

Leaderboard Wireframes

Leaderboard Wireframe #1

Leaderboard Wireframe #2

Leaderboards traditionally is an effective gamification feature that makes users competiive and compare themselevs with others. Users at top rankings feel a sense of accomplishment and recognition and are motivated to keep their ranks. In addition, non-compeittive users enjoy looking at leaderboars out of curioisity, and consume the information presented by them.

I saw an avenue to implement this in the context of a food-delivery app. Imagine seeing your friends is on the top leaderboard for ordering 50 bobas within the span of 2 months! Likewise, seeing oneself on leaderboard provides motivation to maintain the position. Thus, the feautre's goal is to increase user engagement and social aspect of Snackpass by creating conversations between users and providing them more incentives to engage with the app.

For the design, I researched how leaderboards are structured in various apps, particularly gaming apps, to understand the best way to present ranking information. Created two initial sketches and proceeded with designing the second one, as it was more concise and focused in showcasing the information from the first sketch such as rankings, categories, annd specific store the rankings are based by. I borrowed the concept from my first sketch of filtering based on the user's friends or local.

Chicken Coop Readjustment

After conducting the user surveys, I decided to make a mind-map to plan out and brainstorm solutions to key pain-points of the Chicken Coop Feature. From this mindmap, the main-solutions I came up with was:

1. Link the Favorite Store feature to Chicken Coop by providing extra progress to hatching chicks if a user gives the points to their friends favorite store

2. A new gift-card system called SnackCards that allows users to convert coins collected from chickens into free rewards and discounts

Final Prototype Showcases

With the wirefames and mind-maps completed I went ahead and completed the high-fidelity prototypes and linked the flows! This resulted in a completed product that is consistent with the fun and bright color-scheme that Snackpass already has, but still simple so core-functionalities of the app are not hindered.

Reflection

This was my first experience in making a personal project and taking control of understanding and designing a product to imrpove it! I had a great learning experience in teaching myself Figma and understanding all the nunances behind the software. I also reseach mobile app design techniques and accesibility concepts to make sure that the designs I were making were as feasible as they could be.

An aspect of the project I would have done differently if I had time, is interview more business owners! Users like me are one-side of the coin, so I would want to survey restaurant partners with Snackpass how the rewards system affects their business to see if an more effective middle-ground can be achieved.

Regardless, I am super proud of what I was able to achieve and my ability to learn new techniques and push myself out of my comfort zone. Since this was a personal project, I designed all assets and aspects of the project on my own alongst managing my coursework. However, I ket pushing since I know the end-result of redesigning and thinking strategically on how to improve a product I use often.
